The Challenge: Why Two Methods?

  • Silhouette Score (peaks at k=8): Statistical measure of cluster separation

  • Business Context (k=5): Practical interpretability and strategy differentiation

Elbow Method: Inertia Analysis

What is Inertia?

Inertia = Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (WCSS). It measures how tightly packed points are within each cluster. Lower is better - indicates cohesive clusters.

Silhouette Score Method

What is Silhouette Score?

Measures how well each point fits in its cluster vs. neighboring clusters. Formula: (b(i) - a(i)) / max(a(i), b(i))

  • Range: -1 to 1
  • 1.0: Perfect clustering (each point is in right cluster)
  • 0.0: Overlapping clusters (point could be in either)
  • -1.0: Misclassified (point is in wrong cluster)
  • Typical: 0.3-0.7 is good for behavioral data

Why They Disagree

  • Elbow Method: Focuses on variance explained. Looks at absolute improvement in model fit.
  • Silhouette: Focuses on cluster quality. Looks at relative quality of cluster assignments.
  • Different metrics, different answers: Both are mathematically correct; they're optimizing different objectives.
