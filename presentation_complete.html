Optimal k Selection: Elbow Method (Primary Approach)

What is the Elbow Method?

The Elbow Method analyzes Inertia (Within-Cluster Sum of Squares) - how tightly packed points are within each cluster. Lower inertia = better clusters. The method looks for the "elbow" where inertia reduction plateaus.

Elbow Method Visualization

Why k=5 is the Elbow?

  • 15.5% improvement at k=5 - Last major gain in cluster tightness
  • Drop to 12.3% at k=7 - Clear diminishing returns after k=5
  • Rate of change plateaus - 15.5% → 18.9% (k=6) → 12.3% (k=7) shows the inflection
  • Cost-benefit sweet spot - Adding 6th cluster gives 18.9% gain but adds complexity; gains drop to 12% after
📊 Elbow Method Verdict: k=5 is the optimal point where cluster quality improvements start to plateau relative to added complexity. This is the classic "elbow" inflection point in clustering analysis.

Optimal k Selection: Silhouette Score Method (Validation)

What is Silhouette Score?

Measures how well each point fits in its cluster versus neighboring clusters. Range: -1 to 1 (higher = better separation).

Why k=5 Over k=8?

  • Elbow Method: Clear elbow at k=5 (15.5% improvement, then diminishing)
  • Business Context: 5 distinct, manageable strategies; k=8 too granular
  • Silhouette Trade-off: k=5 is 0.353 vs. k=8 peak of 0.364 (+3%) - marginal gain
  • Execution Feasibility: 5 segments implementable; 8 segments = management complexity overhead

Validation & Limitations

Validation: Business validation (each cluster has distinct profile), Stability check (consistent across runs), Statistical validation (ANOVA p<0.001).
Limitations: Snapshot data (not time-series), behavioral/financial features only (no demographics), 4.9% outliers removed, tenure ~12 months (less developed for newer customers).
