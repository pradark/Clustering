Technical Details & Cluster Validation

Clustering Algorithm Specifications

Algorithm: K-Means with k-means++ initialization
Library: scikit-learn (Python)
Random State: 42 (reproducibility)
Number of Initializations: 20
Distance Metric: Euclidean
Convergence Tolerance: 1e-4

Feature Scaling & Normalization

Method: StandardScaler (Z-score normalization)

Formula: x_scaled = (x - mean) / std_dev

Purpose: Ensure equal weighting of all features regardless of original scale. Without normalization, high-magnitude features (like BALANCE in dollars) would dominate the algorithm.

Silhouette Analysis Results

Silhouette Score Formula: For each point i, s(i) = (b(i) - a(i)) / max(a(i), b(i))

  • a(i): Average distance from point i to other points in same cluster
  • b(i): Average distance from point i to points in nearest cluster
  • Range: -1 to 1, where 1 = perfect clustering, 0 = overlapping, -1 = misclassified

Our Result: Silhouette Score = 0.357 (good cluster separation, expected for behavioral data)

Why k=5?

Silhouette scores across k values:

  • k=2: 0.283 - Too coarse, loses important business distinctions
  • k=3: 0.297 - Still combines dissimilar behaviors
  • k=4: 0.314 - Moderate improvement
  • k=5: 0.357 ✓ OPTIMAL - Best business interpretation, good statistical separation
  • k=6: 0.363 - Slightly higher score but introduces complexity without business benefit
  • k=7: 0.360 - Diminishing returns, less interpretable

Stability Check: k-means++ initialization ensures consistent cluster assignment across runs. We ran 20 initializations and verified identical cluster memberships.

Limitations & Considerations

  • Snapshot Data: Analysis based on historical aggregate data, not time-series. Doesn't capture recent behavior changes.
  • Feature Availability: Clustered only on behavioral/financial features. Demographic data (age, income, location) not included - could improve segmentation.
  • Outlier Removal: Removed 4.9% of observations as anomalies. These customers may represent unique segments but were excluded to stabilize clustering.
  • Tenure Impact: Most customers have ~12 months history. New customer patterns less developed; may improve with longer observation periods.
  • External Validation: Ideally would validate against future performance (defaults, churn, profitability). Historical backtesting recommended before full implementation.
